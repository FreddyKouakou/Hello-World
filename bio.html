Retirement Bio

Freddy Kouakou was born in Cote d’Ivoire in West Africa. He joined the prestigious BYU Idaho University in 2017 and graduated with BS in software engineering in 2021. His love for teaching grew from his teaching experience in private schools in Ghana for 5 years. He created TheYoungIvorianSoftDev institution, which has trained and continue to train thousands of young Ivorian to the software development industry. Freddy used to say he dropped into software development from the window, and made it a mission to open the gate of the software industry to many young men and women in his country as well as many others across Africa. Freddy created and managed many mobile and web applications useful in various industries such as eLessonPlan, developed as a school project which brought change in Ghana’s education system by improving teachers and student performances. Freddy earn many awards for his impact in education in Africa, gave many talks and participated in many worldwide conferences. He wrote many news articles and authored or coauthors many tech books. His first tech articles were published in his own digital newspaper cofounded with his BYU Idaho course mates and is the number one online tech news in Cote d’Ivoire. His best achievement was his service for the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints as mission president after serving at various position in his local community. Freddy has retired from his many businesses and consecrate his time to his family, and his experience as BYU Idaho online instructor.
